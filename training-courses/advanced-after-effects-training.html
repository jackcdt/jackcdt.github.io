After Effects Advanced Course

This two day advanced class is a follow-on course to the After Effects Essentials course, with students learning how to use Adobe After Effects to it’s full potential.

Learn about advanced 3D kinetic typography and infographic techniques, using expressions, audio and linking vision to audio, working across multiple compositions. Industry standard tips and tricks for colour correction and special effects are some of the key learnings in this course.

Special emphasis is placed on new Creative Cloud features, which are updated every two months. Best practice workflow for advanced 3D compositing using the Cineware Plugin for Cinema 4D Lite will be covered, along with a solid introduction to Cinema 4D Lite.

Our instructors are Adobe Certified, industry experienced and passionate about training! We recommend using the latest version - After Effects CC 2014 (Creative Cloud), but we also have CS6 and CS5 options. Not sure which version? See our article on Adobe software versions.

At City Desktop Training – an Adobe Authorised Training Centre – upon course completion participants receive a Certificate endorsed by Adobe.

Advanced Animation Techniques

  • Keyframe interpolation (linear, auto bezier, hold and
  • roving keyframes)
  • The graph editor
  • Separate x and y and z dimensions
  • Auto orient along a path

Character Animation

  • Character rigging using advanced parenting chains
  • Using the pan behind tool to move the anchor point
  • Using null objects
  • Pre-Composing/nesting

Using the Roto brush

  • Roto brush
  • Refine edge tool
  • ▪Refine feather tool (CC)

Advanced 3D

  • Animating the camera with a null object
  • Camera depth of field and material options
  • Creating simple 3d objects and scenes
  • Snapping in the viewer
  • Precomposing 3D layers and Collapse Transformations
  • Auto orient layers towards camera
  • Nesting and collapse transformations
  • Multiple view windows locked to individual comps
  • Advanced motion blur settings

Animating a Photograph in 3D - 'Parallax Effect'
