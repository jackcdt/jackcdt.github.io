Course Outline

Section 1: Introduction to SEO

Jumping head first into the semester, we start by covering a massive overview of SEO fundamentals and best practices. Included in this section is a history of SEO, how search engines work, and an overview of the top ranking factors. For those new to SEO, this is a critical building block for your SEO training.

Section 2: On-Page Best Practices

The on-page factors of a website should adhere to a long list of best practices that make that website more appealing and readable to the search engines. In this module, Todd dissects a website and identifies 20 signals of an optimised page. He then introduces some best practices to follow regarding basic on-page fundamentals. These best practices include writing effective titles and META descriptions, creating optimised URLs, and making copy relevant and accessible. Get started with SEO by gaining an understanding of just what the search engines look for when they crawl your website.

Section 3: Keyword Research

The cornerstone of SEO rankings is clearly found in keyword research. Without a good foundation for finding, analysing, and selecting keywords for your site, your rankings will fall short of desired expectations and you'll leave legitimate customers searching unsuccessfully for your site.

In this section we address this very topic. Follow along as Todd and Matt show you some great tools and solid strategies for quality keyword research.

Section 4: Competitive Analysis

By now you should have a good understanding of the possibly the most important component in developing your SEO strategy: Keyword Research. This next step in your initial research and planning process is Competitive Analysis. This second piece of the puzzle will enable you to evaluate your keyword decisions outside of the vacuum and look at them in relation to your competition. This will give you a good idea of how much success you can expect to have with regards to your competition, and thus, where your SEO efforts should focus.

Section 5: Design and Architecture

This next section builds upon what was already talked about with keyword research, and addresses another of the more critical components of SEO success: site architecture. Designing and architecting your site correctly the first time is of critical importance. Matt Bailey and Todd Malicoat explain some very complex ideas in this module that can significantly improve your SEO.

Section 6: Site Optimisation and Best Practices

On-page optimisation is probably what most people think of when they think of SEO.  From well-formed titles to internal linking strategy to a comprehensive lecture on smart page copy techniques, this module will cover all things on-page.

Additionally, this section also includes a critical video on duplicate content. If your site is blog-based or you work with a CMS, this video will be especially important for you.

Section 7: Link Building

Your site is researched, designed, and built. Now you can sit back and watch the traffic flow, right? Not so fast. As you saw in the first section, good rankings come not only from a well-designed site, but also from the inbound links to your site. In this section, Todd illustrates how all links are not created equally, and shows you the best way to go about getting those perfect incoming links.

After completing the training video, review the study guide and complete the assignment.

Section 8: SEO for Local Search

Most of the time, your SEO efforts are directed toward global optimisation. And most of the time, especially if your company markets to the global economy, that's where all your efforts should go. But what if you have a strong local presence and want to target customers in your community?

In this section, join Todd for a discussion of the top factors to consider when optimising for local search results.

Section 9:Conducting SEO Site Audits

A site audit is one of the most requested services of an SEO expert. In a site audit, an SEO will run through sometimes hundreds of check points to find potential problem areas that negatively affect ranking. Then, the problem spots are prioritised and fixed accordingly.

Experienced SEOs have lists of checkpoints to cover, and in this module you'll find Todd's own personal list as well as a run through of the step-by-step process for conducting a thorough site audit.

Section 10: Critical Algorithm Updates

With the Panda update and more recent Penguin update, it is important to stay on top of what is changing with the algorithm.  These videos should keep up in the loop.

Section 11: SEO Tracking

Even with all the SEO optimisation in place, an SEO's work is not done.  Clients will request site audits, performance metrics, and additional data.  As a result, all internet marketers should be familiar with some critical knowledge of running SEO analytics, setting realistic goals, and running an impromptu site audit on an existing or new web property.

In this module, Todd explains all these concepts and gets you prepared to go into any company or client meeting with some additional essential tools under your belt.

Section 12: SEO Career Path

Learning SEO skills is half the battle. The other half is figuring out where you'll be applying those skills. Generally speaking, there are three paths to go with SEO training: 1) You can choose to be an independent SEO consultant. 2) You can work in-house for a company doing SEO on corporate sites. 3) You can work for an agency that helps other companies.

Section 13: SEO Tools

One of the many time-saving strategies SEOs have is using the right tool for the right task.  In this section, we've put together a series of videos that will help shape a lot of your future SEO efforts.  SEOs are very loyal to their tools, so from the many options available, you'll be able to find tools that work best for you.
