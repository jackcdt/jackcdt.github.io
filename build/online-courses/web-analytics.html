Web Analytics

Course Outline

Section 1: Introduction to Web Analytics

The course kicks off with a series of introductory videos that should ease your way into the world of Web Analytics, including a rather comprehensive overview on data collection. Avinash's concepts are inspirational, approachable, and concrete, giving you the opportunity to master what is traditionally a very complex field of study.

Section 2: Data Collection

You know you need the data. But where will it come from, and how comprehensive can it really be? In this compelling series, Avinash explains the eight methods of collecting data from your blog, website, or mobile app. Whether you're a developer, a site owner, or a business professional, you need to understand the breadth and depth of data available, so you can make informed decisions. This series gives you not only the knowledge you need, but the code to make it happen.

Section 3: Key Performance Indicators & Analytics Techniques

Defining useful metrics are absolutely critical to successful analytics. Yet often times, it's difficult to know exactly which metrics are the most effective...and which ones are a waste of time. For years, analysis revolved around tracking total and unique visitors, but fortunately, things have become a great deal more sophisticated. In this first section, Avinash begins our analytics training with a comprehensive introduction to KPIs, giving us a solid foundation from which base out advanced analytics strategies.

Section 4: Segmentation

From time on site to referring sites to organic vs paid traffic, understanding your visitors and the way they interact with your website is crucial to optimising your pages and ultimately, to converting those visitors into customers. Partnered with well thought out KPIs and your data analysis feeds you information that will transform your web strategies. In this course, follow along as analytics guru and segmentation evangelist, Avinash Kaushik, walks you through good data, bad data, and the importance of effective segmentation analysis.

Section 5: Reports and Metrics: Making Web Analytics Actionable

Okay, now that we've got a firm handle on segmentation and KPIs, reports should be relatively easy to understand, at least on a conceptual level. And yet, as with all analytics, the concept suddenly becomes a slippery slope.

In this next set of videos, Avinash delves into reports, SEO analytics, and path analysis with his usual zeal and manages, once again, to take a broad, abstract topic and reduce it down to elements we can understand and put into practice immediately.  With this knowledge, you'll be ready to create effective custom reports and analyse business impact on a level that significantly improves the bottom line.

Section 6: Web Data Analysis Guide

In this next, extremely valuable module, Avinash offers a comprehensive overview of web data analysis. From visitor orbit to micro conversions to your company's measurement model to strategies for doing a blind data analysis, Avinash takes you through a detailed overview of some very effective data analysis techniques and strategies.

After a clear understanding of KPIs and Segmentation, this video series should take you through the next stage in analysing your critical data.

Section 7: Qualitative Research

Website surveys are an intriguing opportunity for your website. And surprisingly, they remain a very effective manner in which to assess your users' needs and wants. Still not a believer? You haven't heard Avinash expound on the greatness of the elusive survey.

Section 8: Experimentation and Testing

Testing. Testing. Testing. If you're not testing, you're doing yourself and your website a disservice. If you've watched any of Bryan Eisenberg's videos, you know how important it is to continue to test and try different things. Avinash is no different...and since you're already following your analytics, setting up testing is a no-brainer. So take your segmentation and KPI knowledge and put it to work.

Section 9: Competitive Intelligence Analysis

Over the past several weeks, Avinash has described many ways in which you can – and should – analyse the data on your own website. This week, we'll branch out to an equally important strategy – understanding how to collect and analyse data on your competitor's websites. Armed with this information, you can take your strategic decisions out of the vacuum and analyse exactly what keywords you can compete for, further driving the arrows on your dashboards up and to the right.

Section 10: Multi-Channel Analytics

Avinash Kaushik introduces you to the wonderland that is multi-channel analytics in the first video in a four part series. It's a daunting topic, one that most online marketers prefer to avoid, but Avinash tackles it head on by addressing the primary challenge in connecting offline activity to online data.

Section 11: Supplemental Materials: Dashboards

With KPI's providing insights on exactly how your company is performing, it is vital that this data be assembled in a way that makes it easily viewable and thus actionable. Use dashboards to consolidate such data in effective ways that influence decision makers to take action and ultimately affect the bottom line.

Section 12: Supplemental Materials: Industry Models, Complex Measurement, and Approaches

Supplemental Materials: Industry Models, Complex Measurement, and Approaches
This module includes a number of videos that don't fit well in other modules yet offer valuable additional information on analytics measurement and strategies. There is no assignment attached to these videos, but questions based on this video content may appear on the final test.

Section 13: Supplemental Materials: Social Media Analytics

One of the more requested sets of information these days is Social Media measurement.  What should you measure, how do you measure success, and how do you include economic impact?  All these questions and more are answered in this series.  There is no assignment attached to these videos, but questions based on this video content may appear on the final test.

Section 14: Supplemental Materials: Google-Specific Techniques and Reports

This module introduces information specific to Google Analytics data analysis. While not every company utilises GA, it is the most widely used tool and important for students to effectively navigate around. There is no assignment attached to these videos, but questions based on this video content may appear on the final test.
