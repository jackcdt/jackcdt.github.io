Course Outline

Section 1: Introduction to Social Media

Social Media is hot ... and continues to get hotter.  Companies no longer question whether Social Media can work for them;  now, it's about how it can work for them.  With an ever-growing list of channels and paths, Social Media can quickly spin out of control.  In this comprehensive introduction to the discipline, Jennifer covers the vast spectrum of Social Media options, getting you prepared for taking advantage of the massive opportunities in front of every business.

Section 2: Creating a Social Media Strategy

In many ways social media is still in its Wild Wild West stages, with ever-changing sites and services, and an even bigger array of options for leveraging them. It can be confusing, and without a well-thought-out plan, your time and resources can quickly drain. Alternatively, a well-conceived social media campaign can create tremendous levels of success.

These introductory videos by Jennifer Cario will clarify the social media landscape and help you build a strategy to make social media work for you.

Section 3: Understanding Paid, Earned, and Owned Social Media

The concepts of paid, earned, and owned have been part of the traditional marketing lexicon for decades. Now in the online realm, these three types of media are forced to work together as messages spread online. In this section, Jennifer defines all three types of media, and she shows a step-by-step marketing plan that ingeniously leverages all three media types. The fast-paced world of Social Media deserves a media strategy that deliberately and thoughtfully leverages the three media types in an effort to amplify and spread a message.

Section 4: Blogging for Business

You could say that nothing has pervaded the web so quickly and effectively as the blog. Thanks to ease of use, extensive reach, and the embrace of every aspect of the web community (including search engines), the value and authority factor of blogs continues to grow.

But without a strategic plan, blogging can quickly become an unfocused drain on resources with little measurable return. Whether you're running your own personal blog, a corporate blog, or looking for ways to leverage popular blogs to your benefit, this section will get your strategy in line.

Section 5: Viral Marketing

Everyone talks about "going viral", but few actually succeed at making it work. It's hard to know exactly what "viral" really means in the Internet world, how to establish what you can do to start a viral marketing campaign, how to get your campaign out to the masses, how to measure its success, and how to learn from other people's mistakes.

In this section, Jennifer takes a close look at all these issues, and gives you the tools and information you need to create your  own overnight marketing coup.

Section 6: Finding and Communicating with Your Influencers

You've done all this great work getting your videos built; your Twitter account is humming along; your Facebook page is stocked with valuable goodies for your visitors; and your blog is fully integrated with your other campaign elements. So how come the cash register remains idle? There's still one critical step in getting your product or service to be the talk of the town: getting it in front of the right people. Even the best campaigns and products need that extra boost that only far-reaching influencers can provide.

In this module, Jennifer discusses the importance of influencers, and how to go about vetting and communicating with the people that can make the biggest impact for your company.

Section 7: Video and YouTube

According to Comscore, almost 150 million users watch nearly 13 million videos every month. Those numbers only continue to grow, and video is increasingly adopted as a standard means of accessing both entertainment and information for Internet users. That means integrating video into your marketing efforts is more important with every passing day.

Video marketing expert Greg Jarboe explains the video options available, how YouTube works, and how to take advantage of YouTube as a social media marketing tool.

NOTE:  The videos in this module are part of a bigger series, but not all are required for this course.  For in-depth, comprehensive YouTube marketing training, jump over to our YouTube Video Marketing course.

Section 8: YouTube Advertising

While YouTube is widely known as Social Media marketing channel, there is another side of YouTube that many marketers are taking advantage of:  YouTube advertising.  From in-video ads to more traditional on-page ads, marketers are making good money by creating engaging content and driving visitors directly to their channels.  In this module, Greg Jarboe explores the multitude of options available to marketers who want to take advantage of the other side of the YouTube funnel.

Section 9: Social Networking and Facebook

Facebook is not just for baby pictures any more. In this module, Jennifer uncovers not only the social networking opportunities for individuals but how businesses can take a simple networking application and turn it into a massive lead generation and customer communication platform. From custom landing pages to groups to apps and tagging, you'll learn all the ins and outs of this versatile and far-reaching social networking application.

Section 10: Facebook Advertising

Facebook likely has more data about individual users than any other online entity. The social network tracks users' page likes, interests, gender, location, and various other types of information. This vast amount of data presents an extraordinary opportunity for marketers to target specific sets of highly relevant users. Further, Facebook's advertising options can accomplish a variety of tasks including encouraging page likes or driving visitors to a website.

In this module, Jennifer covers the gamut of options available when advertising across Facebook. Learn the various goal-focused ad options as well the expansive list of targeting options.

Section 11: Microblogging and Twitter

Everybody else is doing it. Why shouldn't we? That's certainly the question a lot of businesses are asking right now. In reality, the question should be more along the lines of, "Everybody else is doing it. SHOULD we also?" Often, companies jump on board with the latest fad before considering whether it's the right tool for their business - or before they have any sort of strategy at all.

In this section, Jennifer explains strategies, tips, best uses, and plenty of tools surrounding this versatile business tool. At the end, you should have all the information you need to figure out how Twitter can be a valuable marketing tool for your business.

Twitter's user-generated conversation is fluid and rapid, and it can be a challenge for brands to get their message to stand out. Advertising across the microblogging platform can enable brands to get their message seen. Twitter ads enable longer messaging, increased engagement, and a less time-constrained delivery. Throughout this module, gain an understanding of how to leverage Twitter's advertising options, and create a powerful ad campaign that doesn't get lost in the stream of short messages.

Section 12: Online Reputation Management

In the real world, if someone says something negative about your company or your product, it generally gets lost in a sea of consumer white noise and reaches relatively few people. On the Internet, however, bad press is aggregated into all other information about your company. A simple Google search for your brand can yield all kinds of nastiness, whether valid or not.

At one time or another, every company is faced with a disgruntled customer, but successful companies are well prepared for how to manage those complaints and deal with them head on, often saving considerable revenue from going down the toilet. In this module, Jennifer explains how to look for - and address - bad reviews and comments about your company, empowering you to take control of your online reputation.

Section 13: Social Media Measurement

So you're probably already participating across half a dozen Social Media channels, but what impact has this made on your business? If you're not measuring the direct effects of your social media efforts, you may be unaware of how, or if, you're affecting the company's bottom line. It's essential to measure various social metrics to understand how your efforts are actually driving conversions, leads, and sales. 

In this module, Jennifer identifies the key metrics to track when measuring Social Media as well as how to tie those metrics directly to real business outcomes.

One of the more requested sets of information these days is Social Media measurement.  What should you measure, how do you measure success, and how do you include economic impact?  All these questions and more are answered in this series.  There is no assignment attached to these videos, but questions based on this video content may appear on the final test.

Section 14: Marketing Through LinkedIn

LinkedIn has grown to be a leader in professional networking. Now, companies are leveraging the power of LinkedIn to effectively build and manage B-to-B relationships, and using it as a powerful lead generation tool.

In this module, Jennifer introduces you to LinkedIn and explores some ways in which you can take advantage of its business marketing opportunities.

Section 15: Pinterest and Image Marketing

Pinterest is a powerful new marketing channel that requires serious strategy. It's a community of people predisposed to collect information, who have defined their areas of interest, and who have volunteered to share. So if you can find your audience (and its influencers) and provide them with great content, they'll engage with you, and help you find more prospects. Jennifer breaks down Pinterest marketing into easily relatable business concepts, giving you a framework to help build your strategy. Jen also shows you how to set up analytics to track and evaluate your efforts, so you can see if you're headed in the right direction, and respond early and authoritatively if change is required.
