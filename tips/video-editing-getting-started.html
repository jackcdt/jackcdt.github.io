    What are motion graphics?

    Motion graphics is the art of animating graphics; titles (and sometimes audio and video) to convey information in a visually appealing way. Previously, before software like Adobe Affects existed, producing motion graphics were limited to television production and high budget filmmaking.

    Common motion graphic projects are:

    • Kinetic Typography (animated titles)
    • Advertisements
    • Info graphics/ charts
    • Animated logos

      Learn motion graphic skills in our Adobe After Effects Essentials course.

    What are visual effects?

    Visual effects are used in the film and TV industries to enhance real filmed footage that may not be possible to film on set. This could be the simulation of explosions, fireworks gunfire etc. Visual effects are often used in conjunction with CGI (computer generated images) and cover many disciplines including:

  • Compositing: Layering actors filmed in front of a green screen with background footage etc.
  • Particle effects: Simulating real world physical behaviour (such as fire or water).
  • Colour correction/grading
  • 3D animation: Creating 3D objects and characters using computer software.

    •   An introduction to the above skills and more are covered in the Adobe After Effects Advanced course.
