Landing Page Conversion & Optimisation

Getting users to your site is only half the battle. Getting them to purchase, to register, to fill out your form, to convert them from visitors to customers — that is your end goal. Bryan Eisenberg and Avinash Kaushik provide a comprehensive overview of landing page optimisation, and take you through some real-world examples of what works, what doesn't, and what steps you can take to, as Bryan says, "always be testing." In addition to video lessons, the course references Bryan Eisenberg's book Always Be Testing. Supplementing the video lessens with the book is highly recommended to enhance the student learning experience, but is not an absolute requirement to successfully complete the course.

This course qualifies graduates for OMCP® Certification. Recommended prerequisites: Fundamentals: Conversion

Course Outline

Section 1: Introduction to Conversion optimisation

You've brought someone to your website, but what happens next? In our Conversion Optimisation Practitioner program, Bryan Eisenberg teaches you the in's and out's of effective communication with your site visitors and the steps to follow to continually improve conversion rates. Learn what it really means to focus on the consumer, and identify opportunities to create a positive online user experience that will motivate them to take action. Because bringing traffic to your site is only half the puzzle. Getting visitors to purchase, register, or otherwise engage is the next step.

In this introductory overview, you'll get a good feel for what it means to embrace an always be testing culture. In this course, we'll be using video training modules. In addition to video lessons, the course references Bryan Eisenberg's book Always Be Testing, which is highly recommended to enhance your learning experience, but is not required.

Section 2: Conversion Planning

Now that you have a good sense of what conversion optimisation is, what's required to create a solid optimisation culture, and some of the tools available to set up and run your tests, it's time to jump in and develop some concepts and strategies.

In Developing a Framework, you'll learn how to identify and qualify your website traffic so you can better understand which leads are most likely to result in conversions.

Then, you'll dive into one of the more critical aspects of optimisation: customer personas. You'll discover the strategies and goals of each persona, and more importantly, how to build your site so it gives each persona what they need to feel comfortable doing business with you.

Section 3: Conversion Design Concepts

Website designs need to accomplish much more than just look pretty. They need to drive visitors to complete their goals. Encouraging visitors to complete their goals involves using various design and copy elements to drive them in the right direction.

In this module, Bryan explains why the driving concept behind a site design should be moving the customer to the next step in the purchasing process, and he shows how to tailor your website to move the customer forward.

Section 4: Creating Conversion Structure

At this point, you should have a good grasp on not only the concept of personas, but how to identify and design your website around the various personas. Additionally, your first test should now be set up with a solid hypothesis and reasoning behind it.

In this next module, we'll begin to talk about structure, including defining goals, deciding what to test, measuring your results, and quality evaluation of your landing pages. Section 5: Building Momentum

Okay, so you know your users, cater to them all, have the perfect landing pages, and your tests are yielding good results. Now what? Momentum. Keeping your users interested, trusting in you, and consistently moving through your site and through the conversion process are the ultimate goals. In this next module, we'll take a close look at all the things that keep your customers happy and wanting to do business with you. We'll also take a look at the results of our first test and run a second test.

Section 6: Engaging in a Dialogue with Your Users

Communication between you and your customer is a critical part of the success of your business. Everything from the language you use on your website all the way to managing complaints, reviews, and testimonials from existing customers can sway users either way about your products and services.

In this next module, we'll take a look at the multiple ways in which you and your customers communicate with each other.

Section 7: Web Analytics Analysis & Measurement

As we've already started to learn, part of effective Conversion is in understanding the ways in which users interact with our websites. Up until now, we've looked at this data through a single A/B test, allowing us to understand user preferences between two options. As we become more comfortable understanding that useful, yet simple data, we can begin to expand that knowledge into more sophisticated goals and metrics.

Which brings us to Web Analytics... Defining useful metrics is absolutely critical to successful testing. Yet often times, it's difficult to know exactly which metrics are the most effective...and which ones are a waste of time. For years, analysis revolved around tracking total and unique visitors, but fortunately, things have become a great deal more sophisticated.

In this module, Web Analytics faculty chair, Avinash Kaushik, joins the conversation with a comprehensive introduction to KPIs, giving us a solid foundation from which base out advanced analytics strategies.

Section 8: Taking Action with Conversion Strategies

In this last module, Bryan takes all the concepts and strategies and turns them into action items. You'll see 21 tips for top converting websites as well as actionable exercises to take back to your own company on which to capitalise... especially if you are tracking social media trends, which you should.

Section 9: Product Page Optimisation

Worry less about shopping cart abandonment, and pay more attention to product pages. Learn the seven product page essentials you've got to master to compel visitors to make that click. Plus, learn what it is that motivates people to buy your applications or services, and improve your non-ecommerce product pages.

Section 10: PPC Conversion Strategies

Learn how to create a seamless transition from the ad to the page that maintains scent, stays on message, keeps your visitors focused, and helps compel your visitor to convert. Then discover how important it is to design ads that capture the imagination with bold, clear messaging that sets expectations and guides users to conversion. Lastly, take a look at the bad habits that can keep you from building ads and landing pages that convert.

Section 11: Conversion Math Analytics

Which landing page is your best performer? Are you sure? Bryan and guest lecturer, mathematics expert John Quarto-von Tivadar, shows you how to tell if you've got enough impressions and conversion data to be statistically significant. You'll be able to know with certainty which of your pages are performing and which need to be improved - or just plain thrown out.
